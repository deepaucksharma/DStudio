    Pattern Categories¶

    Core Patterns¶

    Fundamental architectural patterns that shape modern distributed systems:

    • Queues & Streaming ⭐ - Decoupling producers from consumers Related: Work Distribution
    • CQRS ⭐ - Command Query Responsibility Segregation (Greg Young, 2010)¹ Related: Law 3 (Emergent Chaos 🌪️) | Truth Distribution
    • Event-Driven Architecture ⭐ - Choreography over orchestration Related: Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️)
    • Event Sourcing ⭐ - State as a sequence of events (Martin Fowler)² Related: State Distribution | Truth Distribution
    • Saga Pattern ⭐ - Distributed transaction management (Garcia-Molina & Salem, 1987)³ Related: Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️) | PayPal Case Study
    • Service Mesh ⭐ - Infrastructure layer for service communication Related: Control Distribution
    • GraphQL Federation - Unified data graph across services
    • Serverless/FaaS ⭐ - Functions as the unit of deployment Related: Work Distribution

    Resilience Patterns¶

    Patterns that ensure systems survive failures:

    • Circuit Breaker - Preventing cascade failures (Nygard, 2007)⁴ Related: Law 1 (Correlated Failure ⛓️) | DynamoDB Case Study
    • Retry & Backoff ⭐ - Intelligent retry strategies Related: Law 1 (Correlated Failure ⛓️)
    • Bulkhead ⭐ - Failure isolation through partitioning (Nygard)⁴ Related: Law 1 (Correlated Failure ⛓️)
    • Timeout ⭐ - Bounded wait times for operations Related: Law 2 (Asynchronous Reality ⏳)
    • Health Check - Service liveness and readiness Related: Law 5 (Distributed Knowledge 🧠)
    • Graceful Degradation - Reduced functionality under stress Related: Law 6 (Cognitive Load 🤯)
    • Rate Limiting ⭐ - Protecting from overload Related: Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️)
    • Load Shedding - Dropping work to survive Related: Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️)

    Data Patterns¶

    Managing data in distributed environments:

    • CDC (Change Data Capture) ⭐ - Real-time data synchronization Related: State Distribution
    • Tunable Consistency ⭐ - Flexible consistency guarantees (Dynamo)⁵ Related: Truth Distribution | DynamoDB Case Study
    • Sharding ⭐ - Horizontal data partitioning Related: Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️) | State Distribution
    • Caching Strategies ⭐ - Multi-level cache hierarchies Related: Law 2 (Asynchronous Reality ⏳)
    • Geo-Replication ⭐ - Global data distribution Related: Law 2 (Asynchronous Reality ⏳) | Uber Case Study
    • Outbox Pattern ⭐ - Reliable event publishing Related: Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️)

    Coordination Patterns¶

    Patterns for distributed coordination and messaging:

    • Leader Election ⭐ - Single coordinator selection (Raft/Paxos)⁶⁷ Related: Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️) | Truth Distribution
    • Distributed Lock - Mutual exclusion across nodes Related: Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️)
    • Idempotent Receiver ⭐ - Handling duplicate messages Related: Law 3 (Emergent Chaos 🌪️)
    • Service Discovery - Dynamic service location Related: Control Distribution

    Operational Patterns¶

    Patterns for running systems in production:

    • Observability - Metrics, logs, and traces Related: Law 5 (Distributed Knowledge 🧠) | Control Distribution
    • Auto-Scaling ⭐ - Dynamic resource adjustment Related: Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️) | Law 7 (Economic Reality 💰) | Intelligence Distribution
    • Load Balancing - Request distribution strategies Related: Law 2 (Asynchronous Reality ⏳) | Law 4 (Multidimensional Optimization ⚖️) | Work Distribution
    • Edge Computing ⭐ - Processing at the periphery Related: Law 2 (Asynchronous Reality ⏳) | Uber Case Study

    Real-World Pattern Usage¶

    Circuit Breaker at Netflix¶

    Netflix's Hystrix library⁹ implements circuit breakers that handle over 100 billion thread-isolated and 10 billion semaphore-isolated command executions per day. When a service fails, Hystrix prevents cascading failures by "opening the circuit" and serving fallback responses.

    CQRS at LinkedIn¶

    LinkedIn uses CQRS¹⁰ to separate their write-heavy profile updates from read-heavy timeline generation, allowing them to optimize each path independently and serve billions of timeline reads daily.

    Event Sourcing at Walmart¶

    Walmart's order management system¹¹ uses event sourcing to track the complete history of each order, enabling them to reconstruct any order state and handle complex scenarios like partial refunds and multi-step fulfillment.

    Service Mesh at Lyft¶

    Lyft's Envoy proxy¹² (which became the foundation for Istio) handles over 100 million requests per second across their microservices, providing load balancing, circuit breaking, and observability without changing application code.

    Pattern Selection Framework¶

    1. Problem Fit - Does it solve your actual problem?
    2. Complexity Cost - Can your team operate it?
    3. Performance Impact - What's the overhead?
    4. Economic Viability - Is it cost-effective?
    5. Future Flexibility - Does it lock you in?

    Anti-Patterns to Avoid¶

    • Pattern Cargo Cult - Using patterns because others do
    • Premature Distribution - Distributing before necessary
    • Consistency Theater - Over-engineering consistency
    • Resume-Driven Architecture - Choosing for career reasons
    • Infinite Scalability - Ignoring practical limits

    Pattern Learning Path¶

    🌱 Beginner Path (0-2 years experience)¶

    1. Start here: Timeout → Retry & Backoff - Basic resilience
    2. Next: Caching Strategies - Immediate performance benefits
    3. Then: Rate Limiting - Essential protection
    4. Foundation: Load Balancing - Fundamental scaling pattern

    🌳 Intermediate Path (2-5 years experience)¶

    1. Data patterns: CQRS → Event Sourcing
    2. Integration: Event-Driven Architecture → Saga Pattern
    3. Reliability: Outbox → Idempotent Receiver
    4. Infrastructure: Service Mesh → Auto-scaling

    🌲 Advanced Path (5+ years experience)¶

    1. Complex data: Sharding → Geo-Replication → Tunable Consistency
    2. Global scale: Edge Computing → CDN Strategies
    3. Modern architectures: Serverless → GraphQL Federation
    4. Operations: Observability → FinOps

    Key Takeaways¶

    📚 Universal Principles¶

    1. Patterns emerge from constraints - Every pattern solves specific axiom limitations
    2. Trade-offs are mandatory - No free lunch
    3. Context determines choice - Same problem + different constraints = different pattern
    4. Simple beats complex - Start with simplest working solution
    5. Operations are paramount - Must operate at 3 AM
    6. Measure everything - Quantify benefits and costs
    7. Evolution over revolution - Migrate incrementally

    📋 Pattern Selection Checklist¶

    Before: ✓ Constraint mapping ✓ Team readiness ✓ ROI calculation ✓ Fallback plan ✓ Success metrics

    During: ✓ Incremental rollout ✓ Monitoring ✓ Documentation ✓ Team training

    After: ✓ Regular review ✓ Cost tracking ✓ Failure analysis ✓ Evolution planning
