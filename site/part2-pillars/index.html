    Part II: Foundational Pillars¶

    5 patterns that solve 95% of distributed systems problems.

    The Big Idea

    Laws = Physics (what breaks) Pillars = Engineering (how to build anyway)

    Pillar Problem It Solves Key Pattern Real Example
    Work üí™ "Too much for one machine" Load balancing Google processes 100B+ searches/day

    Control üéÆ "How do I manage this mess?" Orchestration Kubernetes: 5.6M developers

    Start with one pillar. Master it. Then add complexity. Most systems fail from premature optimization, not simplicity.

    Deep Dive: The Five Pillars¶

    1. üí™ Work Distribution¶

    One-Line Summary

    Split big jobs into small parallel tasks.

    Theory: Load balancing, queueing theory (M/M/c) Patterns: MapReduce, Load Balancing, Serverless Laws: Asynchrony, Trade-offs

    Quick Decision Guide: 

    CPU-bound? ‚Üí MapReduce
I/O-bound? ‚Üí Async workers  
Bursty? ‚Üí Serverless
Steady? ‚Üí Load balancer

    Example: Google MapReduce (2004) - 20TB across 1800 machines in 30 min¬π

    2. üíæ State Distribution¶

    One-Line Summary

    Keep data alive and accessible at scale.

    Theory: CAP theorem¬≤, consistent hashing Patterns: Sharding, Replication, CDC Laws: Failure, Chaos

    CAP Trade-offs: | Choose 2 | Sacrifice | Example | |----------|-----------|----------| | CP | Availability | Banking | | AP | Consistency | Social media | | CA | Partition tolerance | Single datacenter |

    Example: Netflix Cassandra - 200M users, chose AP over C¬≥

    3. ü§ù Truth Distribution¶

    One-Line Summary

    Get distributed nodes to agree on facts.

    Theory: FLP impossibility‚Å¥, Paxos‚Åµ, Raft‚Å∂ Patterns: Event Sourcing, Saga, 2PC Laws: Asynchrony, Knowledge

    Consensus Comparison: | Algorithm | Fault Tolerance | Complexity | Use Case | |-----------|----------------|------------|----------| | 2PC | None | Low | Same datacenter | | Raft | n/2 - 1 | Medium | Config/metadata | | Paxos | n/2 - 1 | High | Core infrastructure | | PBFT | n/3 - 1 | Very High | Blockchain |

    Example: Google Spanner - TrueTime for global consistency‚Å∑

    4. üéÆ Control Distribution¶

    One-Line Summary

    Keep the circus running without a ringmaster.

    Theory: Control theory, observability Patterns: Service Mesh, Circuit Breakers, Blue-Green Laws: Knowledge, Human API

    Orchestration vs Choreography: | Approach | Control | Flexibility | Debugging | Example | |----------|---------|-------------|-----------|----------| | Orchestration | Central | Low | Easy | Kubernetes | | Choreography | Distributed | High | Hard | Event-driven |

    Example: Kubernetes manages 5.6M developers' apps‚Å∏

    5. üß† Intelligence Distribution¶

    One-Line Summary

    Systems that learn, adapt, and heal themselves.

    Theory: ML systems, chaos engineering Patterns: Auto-scaling, Self-healing, Chaos testing
    Laws: All 7 Laws combined

    Intelligence Maturity Levels: | Level | Capability | Example | |-------|------------|----------| | 1 | Alerts | "CPU > 80%" | | 2 | Auto-scaling | Scale on metrics | | 3 | Predictive | Scale before spike | | 4 | Self-healing | Fix without humans | | 5 | Self-optimizing | Improve over time |
